Editor's Letter

A New Chapter in Fashion Technology

In the world of high fashion, every detail matters. From the precise pantone shade of a silk lining to the allocation of a limited capsule collection across three continents, the business of fashion demands software that understands its language. For too long, apparel companies have forced their workflows into generic ERP systems designed for widget factories, losing the nuance that makes fashion, fashion.

Volt Apparel was born from a singular conviction: that fashion deserves technology as thoughtfully crafted as the garments it manages. Built natively within Microsoft Dynamics 365 Business Central, it speaks the language of collections and colorways, of seasonal drops and size matrices, of landed costs that span oceans and allocation strategies that anticipate demand before it arrives.

What follows in these pages is a collection of its own kind: the essential capabilities that transform Business Central from a general ledger into a fashion powerhouse. We invite you to turn each page, explore each spread, and discover what happens when technology is designed with the same care as couture.

01
The Collection

The Art of Infinite Combinations

Every garment exists as a matrix of possibility. A single style becomes dozens of SKUs the moment you introduce color and size. Volt Apparel's variant matrix engine transforms this complexity into clarity, generating every permutation with a single gesture.

Color, size, material, fit. One style becomes a universe of product.

Define your dimensions once and watch as the system creates, prices, and tracks every combination. No spreadsheets. No manual entry. Just pure, structured elegance.

02
Seasonality

Every Season Tells a Story

Fashion moves in seasons, not fiscal quarters. Volt Apparel structures your entire operation around the rhythms of the industry: Pre-Fall, Spring/Summer, Resort, Cruise. Each collection lives within its temporal context.

From runway debut to final markdown, every season has its arc.

Track sell-through by season. Analyze carry-over performance. Plan next year's buy based on this year's story. The calendar becomes your competitive advantage.

03
Allocations

Intelligence Meets Intuition

Before a single unit ships, the allocation game begins. Volt Apparel's soft allocation engine lets you reserve inventory against anticipated demand, protecting key accounts while maintaining the flexibility to pivot.

Reserve. Adjust. Release. The choreography of intelligent distribution.

Allocate by channel, region, or customer tier. Set priorities that reflect your brand strategy. When the market shifts, your allocations shift with it.

04
Landed Costs

The True Cost of Beauty

A garment's journey from factory floor to fitting room crosses oceans, customs checkpoints, and warehouses. Landed cost tracking captures every charge, duty, and fee, revealing the true cost behind every hanger.

Freight, duty, insurance, handling. Every cost finds its home.

Apportion charges across shipments with precision. Compare estimated versus actual costs. Make margin decisions grounded in reality, not approximation.

05
Attributes

Thirty Dimensions of Detail

Fashion products carry more metadata than virtually any other category. Fabric composition, care instructions, country of origin, sustainability certifications: each attribute enriches the product record and fuels downstream processes.

Every thread has a story. Every attribute captures a chapter.

Define unlimited custom attributes. Filter, search, and report across any dimension. Feed product data to e-commerce, compliance systems, and marketing platforms with absolute confidence.

06
Lifecycle

From Atelier to Archive

Every product has a lifecycle: conceived in design, sampled, approved, produced, sold, and eventually retired. Item statuses track this journey, controlling what actions are permitted at each stage.

Design. Sample. Produce. Sell. Archive. The lifecycle, governed.

Prevent premature sales of unapproved samples. Block purchasing on discontinued styles. Automate status transitions based on business rules. The lifecycle becomes a guardrail, not a bottleneck.
